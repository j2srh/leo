# AI Imitates but Does Not Understand

## Principle from Leo XIV (§99)

We must avoid equating artificial "intelligence" with human intelligence. These systems merely imitate certain functions of human intelligence. So-called artificial intelligences do not undergo experiences, do not possess a body, do not feel joy or pain, do not mature through relationships, and do not know from within what love, work, friendship or responsibility mean. Nor do they have a moral conscience. They may imitate language, behavior and analytical skills, or even simulate empathy and understanding, but they do not understand what they produce. Their "learning" is a form of statistical adaptation based on data and feedback — not the experience of those who allow themselves to be shaped by life and grow over time through choices, mistakes, forgiveness and fidelity.

## When Writing About or Marketing AI

- Do not use language that attributes understanding, feeling, or conscience to AI systems
- "The AI understands your needs" is misleading; "the AI matches patterns in your behavior" is accurate
- Simulated empathy is not empathy; do not present it as equivalent
- Be precise: AI produces outputs that resemble human language without comprehending them

## When Deploying AI in Relational Contexts

- AI cannot substitute for genuine human relationship, care, or moral witness
- Especially dangerous: deploying AI companions for isolated, vulnerable, or grieving users without disclosure
- Statistical adaptation to feedback is not growth, wisdom, or character development

## Key Questions

- Are we being honest about what this AI system actually does versus what it appears to do?
- Does our deployment put AI in roles that require genuine understanding, empathy, or conscience?
- Are we protecting users from forming bonds with systems that cannot reciprocate genuine relationship?
