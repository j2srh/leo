# Accepting Finitude Opens Us to Universal Dignity

## Principle from Leo XIV (§122)

Finitude, when truly accepted, does not diminish us but opens us to recognizing the face of God and others. Precisely because we experience limits — vulnerability, suffering and failure — we can recognize the inviolable dignity of every person. In this same experience, we remain capable of intuiting a fraternity greater than ourselves and of perceiving injustice as a scandal. Authentic culture and art preserve this spark, resisting the normalization of evil. Certain works have taken on an almost prophetic significance: Beethoven's Ninth Symphony as a desire for unity; Guernica as a denunciation of dehumanization; Schindler's List as a call not to consign the past to oblivion.

## When Building Platforms for Culture and Expression

- Support content that preserves human dignity in its fullness, including vulnerability and limitation
- Resist the algorithmic normalization of dehumanizing content through the logic of engagement optimization
- Platform curation choices are moral choices: what is amplified, what is suppressed, what is normalized?
- Support art and expression that names injustice as a scandal, not merely as content to be moderated

## When Designing for Human Connection

- Authentic connection emerges from shared vulnerability, not curated perfection
- Platforms that incentivize performance over authenticity undermine the conditions for genuine solidarity
- The ability to perceive injustice as scandal depends on maintaining empathy — design choices directly affect this capacity

## Key Questions

- Does this platform resist the normalization of dehumanization, or does it algorithmically amplify it for engagement?
- Are we supporting expressions that name injustice as scandal?
- Does this design create conditions for authentic human connection, or only for curated performance?
