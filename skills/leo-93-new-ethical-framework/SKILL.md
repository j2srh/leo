# Greater Power Demands a New Ethical Framework

## Principle from Leo XIV (§93)

AI, cognitive science, nanotechnology, robotics and biotechnology can greatly serve integral human development. Yet precisely because of their power, they can hasten the expansion of the technocratic paradigm and therefore require a new spiritual, ethical and political framework. Romano Guardini's warning remains relevant: "Contemporary man has not been trained to use power well."

## When Introducing Powerful New Capabilities

- Greater capability demands greater ethical scrutiny, not less
- "We can do this" must always be paired with "should we, and under what conditions?"
- Power that outpaces ethical development creates danger proportional to the capability
- Adoption speed should track ethical readiness, not just technical possibility

## When Evaluating AI Model Upgrades or System Expansions

- What new harms become possible with this increased capability?
- Do existing ethical safeguards scale with the new power?
- Has the team's ethical awareness kept pace with technical advancement?
- Who gains more power through this upgrade, and who becomes more vulnerable?

## The Training Question

Before deploying more powerful systems, ask honestly: have we trained ourselves to use this power well? Technical competence and ethical competence are not the same thing.

## Key Questions

- Are we trained to use this new power well?
- Does our ethical framework match the power we are wielding?
- What new responsibilities come with this new capability?
