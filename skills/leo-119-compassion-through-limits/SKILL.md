# Compassion and Wisdom Emerge Through Limitation

## Principle from Leo XIV (§119)

It is precisely within our limitations that compassion finds a place, along with sincere concern for the needs of others; a generosity that can emerge even in the midst of darkness and failure; spiritual experience and the worship of God. We see this at many moments when our limits become tangible: when we face rejection, when we suffer the illness or loss of a loved one, when we encounter our own weakness or failure. Mysteriously, it is precisely in such moments that we can discover a new wisdom, tangibly experience the closeness of others and encounter the presence of the Lord.

## When Designing for Difficult Human Experiences

- Difficult experiences are not UX problems to be optimized away
- Crisis, grief, failure, and limitation are occasions for genuine human connection and depth
- Technology in crisis contexts should enable human connection, not substitute for it
- Speed and efficiency are not the right virtues in experiences of limitation and loss

## When Building Resilience or Support Features

- The most valuable support in limitation is often presence, not solution
- Design features that connect people to other people in difficult moments, not just to AI responses
- Do not present technological answers as substitutes for the wisdom that emerges from lived experience
- Create space for users to encounter their own depth, not just to escape or bypass difficulty

## Key Questions

- Are we treating user difficulty as a problem to solve or as an occasion for growth and connection?
- Does this feature connect people to each other, or substitute for that connection?
- Are we preserving space for wisdom and compassion to emerge from the experience of limitation?
