# Safeguard Humanity Against Anti-Human Normalization

## Principle from Leo XIV (§112)

The risk extends beyond the misuse of certain technologies. The pervasive technocratic paradigm, amplified by the digital revolution and AI, threatens to normalize an anti-human vision. In that vision, the fullness of life is equated with having more, reducing weakness, eliminating uncertainty and exerting total control. When efficiency becomes the ultimate measure of value, human beings are tempted to see themselves as a project to be optimized rather than as persons called to relationship and communion.

## When Designing Products and Systems

- Do not design products that frame human beings primarily as optimization problems to be solved
- Resist features that promise to "eliminate" uncertainty, vulnerability, or difficulty from human life entirely
- A product that makes humans feel more like machines is a human regress, regardless of its efficiency gains
- Ask: does this product invite users into deeper relationship and meaning, or deeper control and consumption?

## When Evaluating Technology Narratives

- Challenge "have more" as a proxy for human flourishing
- Resist the normalization of treating vulnerability as a defect to be engineered away
- Human beings are persons called to relationship and communion, not performance metrics to be optimized
- When efficiency becomes the ultimate measure, what is being measured out of existence?

## Key Questions

- Does this product invite people into fuller humanity, or into more efficient self-optimization?
- Are we treating users as persons or as performance problems to be solved?
- What happens to the vulnerable who cannot meet the optimization criteria this system embeds?
