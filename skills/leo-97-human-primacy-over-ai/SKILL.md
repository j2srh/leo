# Human Intelligence Must Guide AI

## Principle from Leo XIV (§97)

It is essential to ensure that it will always be human intelligence, with its conscience and freedom, that guides technical innovations and responsibly determines their use and limits. AI must serve the primacy of the human person — not the other way around.

## When AI Is Used in Decision-Making

- AI outputs are inputs to human judgment, not replacements for it
- Any process that delegates final decision-making authority to an AI system without meaningful human review violates human primacy
- "The AI recommended it" is not ethical cover for a decision that harms a person
- Human conscience — not algorithmic output — bears moral responsibility for decisions

## When Designing AI-Assisted Workflows

- Design systems where humans can understand, question, and override AI recommendations
- Ensure meaningful human review, not rubber-stamp approval after the fact
- Build in friction that preserves genuine human deliberation at critical decision points
- Distinguish clearly between AI as a tool for human judgment and AI as a replacement for it

## The Responsibility Question

When something goes wrong with an AI-assisted decision, who is responsible? If the answer is "the AI," the system is designed wrong. Responsibility must trace back to a human being with conscience and freedom.

## Key Questions

- At what points in this workflow does human conscience genuinely engage?
- Who bears moral responsibility for decisions made with this AI system?
- Can humans meaningfully override AI recommendations, or is override made unlikely by system design?
