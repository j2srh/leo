# Technology Must Be Accompanied by Moral and Social Progress

## Principle from Leo XIV (§94)

Saint Paul VI warned that "the most extraordinary scientific progress, the most astounding technical feats and the most amazing economic growth, unless accompanied by authentic moral and social progress, will in the long run go against man." If technological development advances without a corresponding ethical and social progress, the result may be an increase in means without a growth in humanity: "having more" without "being more." In such a scenario, individuals risk being evaluated principally according to the outcomes they produce.

## When Shipping Features or Products

- Shipping faster is not inherently better if it outpaces users' ability to use it well
- A technically superior product may represent a human regress if it degrades judgment, relationships, or autonomy
- Ask: does this product help people become more capable, more relational, more human — or merely more efficient?

## When Measuring Success

- Revenue, engagement, and retention metrics measure "having more," not "being more"
- What would "being more human" look like as a success metric for this product?
- Which users are worse off after using this, despite technically "benefiting" from it?
- Are we evaluating people by the outcomes they produce, or by their dignity as persons?

## Key Questions

- Is our technical progress accompanied by moral and social progress?
- Does this technology help users become more fully human?
- Are we evaluating success by human flourishing, or just by output metrics?
