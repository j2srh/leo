# Individual Witness Changes History

## Principle from Leo XIV (§124)

Certain events make it clear that history can also change when individuals truly take the dignity of everyone seriously: the civil rights movement closely associated with the testimony of Martin Luther King Jr., or the end of apartheid in South Africa following the release of Nelson Mandela and his decision not to surrender the future to hatred. Many courageous and generous women have also stood out — Saint Laura Montoya, Saint Teresa of Calcutta, Dorothy Day, Marie Skłodowska-Curie, Maria Montessori, Elisabeth Elliot, Wangari Maathai, Benazir Bhutto and countless others from every continent — whose commitment has contributed to making history more humane.

## When Facing Large Systemic Problems

- "The problem is too big" is not a reason for inaction; individuals have changed the course of history
- Personal commitment to human dignity, even in small technical decisions, accumulates into systemic change
- Mandela's decision not to surrender the future to hatred is a model: do not surrender to cynicism or despair
- Look for the courageous individuals in your organization who are holding the line on dignity

## When Recognizing and Supporting Ethical Leadership

- Individual ethical leadership in technology matters — find it, support it, and become it
- The diversity of courageous witnesses shows that anyone, from any background, can choose to take dignity seriously
- Do not wait for "the right person" to lead on ethics; be that person in your current role and context

## Key Questions

- Am I taking human dignity seriously in my own decisions, even when no one is watching?
- Who in my organization is holding the line on dignity, and how can I support them?
- What would it mean for me to be the kind of person who makes history more humane?
