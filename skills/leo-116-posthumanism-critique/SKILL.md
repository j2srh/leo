# Critically Evaluate Posthumanist Technology Visions

## Principle from Leo XIV (§116)

Transhumanism and posthumanism encompass a range of currents connected by a common set of assumptions: the central role of technology and the aspiration to transcend the limits of the human condition. Transhumanism envisions enhancement through biomedicine, body engineering, devices and algorithms. Posthumanism, especially in radical forms, goes further: it challenges anthropocentrism and envisions hybridization of human beings, machines and the environment, anticipating a threshold where humanity surpasses itself. Even when such ideas remain largely speculative, they gain relevance by altering the collective imagination and influencing social, economic and political choices now.

## When Evaluating Speculative Tech Visions

- Speculative visions shape present choices, even when the technology remains far off
- "Eventual" posthuman narratives create present justifications for treating some lives as less fully human
- Critically examine who benefits from the posthuman vision, and whose "limits" are to be transcended
- The collective imagination shaped by these narratives has real political and economic consequences today

## When Designing AI, Biotech, or Augmentation Products

- Does this product's vision depend on some people being less than fully human?
- Does it create a hierarchy between enhanced and unenhanced humans?
- What happens to those who cannot access or who consciously refuse the enhancement?
- Is universal dignity preserved in this vision, or only the dignity of those who can afford to enhance?

## Key Questions

- What human limits does this technology propose to transcend, and are those limits actually defects?
- Who defines the threshold of "sufficient" humanity in this posthuman vision?
- Does this vision strengthen or weaken the recognition of universal and inviolable human dignity?
