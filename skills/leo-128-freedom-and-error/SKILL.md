# Human Freedom Cannot Be Calculated

## Principle from Leo XIV (§128)

When we embrace the possibility of transcending ourselves through God's grace, we do not deny our nature, nor do we become less human. "We become fully human when we become more than human, when we let God bring us beyond ourselves in order to attain the fullest truth of our being." A technology that merely classifies and optimizes what already exists can, however unintentionally, become an obstacle to change and growth. For an algorithm, an error is a flaw to be corrected; for a person, however, an error can be a catalyst for profound change. A person's future is not calculable, but depends on one's freedom — elevated by the inexhaustible grace of God — and on the relationships cultivated.

## When Designing Predictive or Optimization Systems

- Do not treat the calculable past as the definitive constraint on a person's future
- Predictive systems that assume past behavior determines future capacity deny the possibility of genuine change
- Error, failure, and past data are not a person's destiny — they are part of a story that is not yet finished
- Build systems that keep open the possibility of transformation, not systems that lock people into their past patterns

## When Using AI for Hiring, Credit, Criminal Justice, or Social Services

- A person's past does not fully determine their future; predictive systems that assume otherwise are unjust
- Leave room for human judgment that can recognize and respond to genuine change and growth
- Design exception processes that take seriously the possibility that someone has genuinely transformed

## Key Questions

- Does this system treat human beings as calculable, or does it preserve space for genuine freedom and transformation?
- Can a person outgrow the predictions this system makes about them?
- Are we confusing statistical pattern-matching with understanding a person's actual capacity for growth and change?
