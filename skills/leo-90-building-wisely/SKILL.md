# What Are We Building?

## Principle from Leo XIV (§90)

The biblical contrast between the Tower of Babel and Nehemiah's Jerusalem illuminates our technological moment. Babel represents collective effort following a plan that dominates and ultimately dehumanizes. Jerusalem represents rebuilding piece by piece as a project of shared responsibility. As AI and emerging technologies rapidly transform languages, relationships, institutions and forms of power — we must choose which projects to work on and in what manner, safeguarding the grandeur of humanity given to us as a gift.

## When Designing Systems or Reviewing Architecture

Ask before committing to any design:
- Does this system serve the people who will use it, or does it serve those who control it?
- Who bears the burden of this design, and who captures its benefits?
- Is this a "Tower of Babel" — impressive, centralized, and ultimately dehumanizing — or a "Jerusalem" — built piece by piece with shared responsibility?
- Does this architecture concentrate power or distribute it?

## When Writing or Reviewing Code

- Whose needs are embedded in this code's assumptions?
- Does this feature make users more capable and autonomous, or more dependent?
- Would the people most affected by this system recognize it as serving them?

## Key Questions

- What are we actually building, and for whom?
- Does the technological achievement serve human flourishing or replace it?
- Are the least powerful stakeholders represented in this design?
