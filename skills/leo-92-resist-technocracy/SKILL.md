# Resisting the Technocratic Paradigm

## Principle from Leo XIV (§92)

The technocratic paradigm is the tendency to let the logic of efficiency, control and profit alone shape personal, social and economic decisions. Technology is not simply a tool. When it becomes the standard by which everything is judged, it begins to dictate what matters and what can be discarded — reducing creation to an object of exploitation and human beings to mere cogs in a system driven toward ever greater efficiency.

## When Evaluating Technical Decisions

Challenge every "this is more efficient" or "this optimizes the metric" claim:
- Efficient for whom, and at whose cost?
- What is being discarded or deemed irrelevant by this optimization?
- Is this decision being made because it serves people, or because it serves the system's internal logic?
- Are we letting the algorithm's capabilities determine what we do, rather than letting human needs determine what we build?

## When Designing Metrics and KPIs

- Metrics that can be optimized are not the same as human values
- What cannot be measured by your system may be the most important thing
- Who or what falls outside the measurement system, and what happens to them?

## Key Questions

- Is efficiency the measure of everything here, or just one consideration among many?
- What human realities does this technical approach make invisible?
- Are we treating people as cogs in a system, or as persons with dignity?
