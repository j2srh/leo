# Social Doctrine as a Complete AI Framework

## Principle from Leo XIV (§109)

Social Doctrine principles offer a framework for understanding the AI reality in depth:

- **Common Good**: Exposes new forms of epistemic, economic and political asymmetry — and names the new monopolies of AI
- **Universal Destination of Goods**: Ensuring universal access to both technologies and the education needed to use them
- **Subsidiarity**: Protecting the ability of communities to make choices and corrections, not just observe standards set elsewhere
- **Solidarity**: Recognizing the hidden, often exploited workers who sustain algorithmic systems
- **Justice**: Not only a goal to be safeguarded after technologies are deployed, but a condition that must shape their very design from the outset

## Applying Each Principle in Practice

When reviewing any AI system, work through each principle:

1. **Common Good**: Does this system create or reinforce AI monopolies? Who is named as beneficiary?
2. **Universal Destination**: Who lacks access to this technology or the education to use it effectively?
3. **Subsidiarity**: Can communities correct this system, or only observe and accept it?
4. **Solidarity**: Who are the invisible laborers sustaining this AI pipeline? Are they treated justly?
5. **Justice from the Start**: Was justice a design criterion, or a compliance layer added afterward?

## Key Questions

- Have we applied all five principles, not just the convenient ones?
- Where does this system fail the test of social justice at the design stage?
- Are hidden workers in this AI pipeline recognized and treated with dignity?
