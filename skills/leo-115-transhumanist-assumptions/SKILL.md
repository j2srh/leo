# Identify Transhumanist Assumptions in Tech Culture

## Principle from Leo XIV (§115)

Certain currents of thought interpret progress as surpassing the human condition, often grouped under the labels of transhumanism and posthumanism. These perspectives form the ideological background present in some centers of technological power and occupy the collective imagination in simplified form, especially in media and social networks. They tend to foster enthusiasm for new technologies through a futuristic vision of an "enhanced human being" or "human-machine hybrid." Even when such ideas remain largely speculative, they gain relevance by altering the collective imagination and thereby influence social, economic and political choices.

## When Evaluating Technology Marketing and Vision Statements

- Identify language that frames technology as a path to "transcending" human limits
- Notice when "enhanced humans" or "augmented humanity" narratives appear in product visions
- These narratives are not neutral — they carry assumptions about which human qualities are defects and which are worth keeping
- Naming the ideological background is the first step to evaluating it critically

## When Developing AI Personas, Avatars, or Augmentation Products

- What vision of humanity underlies the design of this "enhanced" product?
- Whose definition of "enhanced" is being applied, and who defines which limits should be transcended?
- Are the imagined benefits of enhancement universally shared, or concentrated among already-advantaged groups?
- What becomes of those who cannot or choose not to enhance?

## Key Questions

- Does this product's vision assume that human limits are defects to be engineered away?
- Whose understanding of the "enhanced human" is this design based on?
- Are we uncritically adopting transhumanist assumptions without acknowledging or examining them?
