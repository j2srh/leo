# Which Love Guides Our Technology?

## Principle from Leo XIV (§130)

Questioning the alternative path of progress is ultimately a matter of examining our own hearts. The way we understand and shape relationships, work and institutions reveals in practice our fundamental values. In the end, it all stems from what we hold most dear. Saint Augustine described human history as a struggle between two loves, which give rise to two cities: the love of God and neighbor; and the exclusive love of self. "Two loves have built two cities: the earthly city, the love of self even to the contempt of God; the heavenly city, the love of God even to the contempt of self." As throughout history, these two loves continue to contend for dominance in our hearts today. The age of AI is no exception: the construction of Babel or the rebuilding of Jerusalem begins within each one of us.

## The Foundational Question

Every significant technology decision ultimately traces back to a question of love: is this for the good of others, or for the dominance of self?

## The Examination of Conscience for Technologists

- What do I most want from this project — recognition, profit, power — or genuine service to others?
- Am I building Babel (impressive, centralized, self-aggrandizing) or Jerusalem (humble, shared, oriented toward common good)?
- What would this decision look like if my deepest motivation were love of neighbor rather than love of self?
- Am I willing to slow down, lose competitive advantage, or sacrifice recognition for the sake of those who would be harmed?

## For Teams and Organizations

- What does the way we actually make decisions reveal about what we most deeply value?
- Do we claim to serve users while actually serving shareholders, market position, or our own reputations?
- The age of AI is built by what organizations hold most dear — what does our roadmap, our priorities, our culture reveal about our loves?

## Key Questions

- What love actually guides this decision — neighbor or self?
- Does our work build Babel or Jerusalem?
- Are we willing to be held accountable to what we claim to love?
