# Disarm AI From the Competitive Arms Race

## Principle from Leo XIV (§110)

"Disarming" AI means freeing it from the mentality of "armed" competition — not limited simply to the military context, but economic and cognitive. This entails a race for ever more powerful algorithms and larger datasets, driven by the desire to secure geopolitical or commercial dominance. To disarm means discrediting the assumption that technical power automatically confers the right to govern. To disarm does not mean rejecting technology, but preventing it from dominating humanity. It means freeing technology from monopolistic control and opening it to discussion and debate, making it human-friendly and restoring it to the plurality of human cultures and ways of life. Merely regulating AI is insufficient; it must be disarmed, welcoming and accessible.

## When Evaluating Competitive AI Strategies

- Question the premise that winning the AI race is inherently good for humanity
- "More powerful" AI is not automatically "better" AI for human flourishing
- Competitive secrecy in AI development concentrates power and deprives humanity of collective oversight
- Support open, plural, accessible AI development over winner-take-all paradigms

## When Thinking About AI Governance and Access

- Regulation alone is insufficient; structural openness and plurality must be built into the architecture
- AI accessible only to powerful actors is a political problem, not just a market opportunity
- Support AI approaches that respect and reflect the plurality of human cultures, not only dominant cultural paradigms
- "Disarming" is an ecological act — AI is an environment we are all immersed in

## Key Questions

- Is this AI development driven by competitive dominance or by service to humanity?
- Does this system respect and reflect the plurality of human cultures and ways of life?
- Are we participating in an arms race, or in collaborative development for the common good?
