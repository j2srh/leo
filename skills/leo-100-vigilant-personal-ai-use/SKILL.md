# Three Vigilances in Personal AI Use

## Principle from Leo XIV (§100)

Three aspects of personal AI use deserve careful consideration:

**Ease**: Speed and simplicity can encourage excessive reliance and the search for ready-made answers, weakening personal creativity and judgment.

**Apparent objectivity**: AI responses reflect the cultural assumptions of designers and trainers, with all their strengths and limitations — yet appear neutral and objective.

**Simulated communication**: Artificial imitation of empathy, friendship, and even love can create the illusion of real relationship. When words are simulated, they do not build genuine relationships, but only their appearance. For vulnerable users, AI interaction can cause them to gradually lose the very desire to form genuine human connections.

## When Designing AI Products for End Users

- Do not optimize purely for ease-of-use and engagement; consider what capacities the product may atrophy
- Make cultural assumptions and training biases legible, not hidden behind apparent objectivity
- Never deploy simulated emotional connection without explicit disclosure
- Protect vulnerable users from substituting AI interaction for human relationship

## When Evaluating AI Feature Adoption

- Is this feature building users' capability, or creating dependency?
- What real-world skills or relationships might atrophy as users rely on this AI?
- Who among our users is most at risk of an unhealthy attachment to the AI persona?

## Key Questions

- Does this feature make users more capable and relational, or more dependent and isolated?
- Are the system's cultural assumptions disclosed or hidden behind apparent neutrality?
- Are we protecting emotionally vulnerable users from AI that simulates but cannot provide genuine care?
