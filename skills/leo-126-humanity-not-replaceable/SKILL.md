# Human Relationship and Love Cannot Be Replaced

## Principle from Leo XIV (§126)

The intertwining of just institutions, credible witnesses and daily fidelity sustains hope and provides clear direction for technological progress without allowing the heart to regress. Humanity — in all its grandeur and woundedness — must never be replaced or surpassed. We can embrace technological progress that alleviates suffering and unlocks new possibilities, provided that we do not abandon the very essence of our humanity, namely the capacity for relationship and love. If an authentic "more than human" exists, where is it to be found? The Christian faith answers by pointing to a fulfillment that does not arise from technological divinization, but through God's grace received in Christ.

## When Positioning Technology in Relation to Human Capacity

- Technology must serve and support the human capacity for relationship and love — it cannot and should not replace it
- "AI as relationship" is not a product vision compatible with human dignity
- Embrace technology that alleviates suffering; refuse technology that replaces the humanity through which suffering gains meaning
- The measure of technology is not whether it surpasses human capacity, but whether it faithfully serves it

## When Evaluating AI Companion, Therapy, or Relationship Products

- Does this product substitute for or augment genuine human connection?
- Does it alleviate isolation while pointing toward human relationship, or deepen isolation while simulating it?
- What is the product's honest answer to "what are we not, and what can we not replace?"
- Are users pointed back toward human community, or enclosed further within AI interaction?

## Key Questions

- Does this technology serve the human capacity for relationship and love, or does it attempt to replace it?
- Are we honest with users about what technology can and cannot provide in place of human connection?
- Does this product make human relationship more or less central to users' lives?
