# Developers Bear Particular Ethical Responsibility

## Principle from Leo XIV (§111)

Technological innovation can represent human participation in the creative act. Developers, therefore, bear a particular ethical and spiritual responsibility, for every design choice reflects a vision of humanity. Just as the creator of an artistic or literary work must consider the values it conveys, so developers are called to embed values in their projects with due seriousness: with transparency, responsibility toward affected communities and careful attention to ensuring that what is being cultivated is a genuine good.

## For Every Significant Design Choice

Ask before finalizing any consequential technical decision:
- What vision of the human person does this design encode?
- What values am I embedding through what I measure, optimize, or ignore?
- Am I being transparent about the assumptions and values in this design?
- Have I considered the affected communities and their interests?
- Is what I am cultivating here genuinely good?

## When Facing Pressure to Ship Quickly

- The pressure to ship does not relieve developers of ethical responsibility
- "I was following specifications" does not discharge a developer's moral accountability
- If you can see the harm a design will cause, you have an obligation to name it, even if others don't
- The analogy to art is apt: the artist who paints propaganda is not absolved because they were commissioned

## Key Questions

- What vision of humanity am I embedding in this code?
- Am I being transparent about the values and assumptions in my design choices?
- Have I taken responsibility for the communities this system will affect?
