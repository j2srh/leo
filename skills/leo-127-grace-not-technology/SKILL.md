# Authentic Transcendence Is Through Grace, Not Technology

## Principle from Leo XIV (§127)

The expression "more than human" is not an exclusive domain of technological promise. For centuries, the Christian tradition has maintained that human beings are not confined by the boundaries of their own nature; rather, they are called to self-transcendence, not through an escape from reality or contempt for their limitations, but through their fulfillment in love. Faith recognizes an openness toward the "beyond" which originates as a gift from God. This process of elevation "surpasses every capability of created nature," for an infinite disparity separates our finite nature from the life of God. Yet it remains possible to enter into the heart of that inexhaustible life. "If anyone is in Christ, there is a new creation: everything old has passed away; see, everything has become new."

## When Technology Claims to Offer Human Transcendence

- Name the Promethean claim: technology that promises to make us "more than human" is making a theological claim, and it deserves theological scrutiny
- Authentic self-transcendence does not come through enhanced self-sufficiency but through a relationship that liberates
- Technology that helps people love more deeply, serve more generously, and encounter the sacred is technology in service of authentic transcendence
- The limit is not an obstacle to transcendence — it is the site where genuine transcendence begins

## When Responding to Technological Utopian Claims

- Ask what kind of transcendence is being offered, and at what cost to those who cannot afford it
- Promethean self-sufficiency creates isolation; authentic transcendence creates communion
- The person enhanced in isolation is not "more than human" — they are less, having sacrificed relationship for capability

## Key Questions

- Is this technology offering authentic transcendence or Promethean self-sufficiency?
- Does it help people love and serve more fully, or does it promise salvation through self-optimization?
- How does this technology relate to the deepest human longing to transcend oneself in love?
